Lipkovo lake

Lipkovo lake is an artificial lake in the north-east part of North Macedonia.The lake resides in The black Mountain of Skopje and was created in 1958 from a dam in the river of Lipkovo, a small branch of river Pcinje.The lake is helped by another neighbouring reservoir located above,the artificial lake of Gleznja.The reservoir is near the village of Lipkovo,after which it took it name.The lake has been constructed so the citizens are able to water the agricultural lands of the region and to secure drinkable water for neighbouring villages.The tubes take the water to the city of Kumanovo,up to 20 kilometers away.The dam is also equipped with a small hydropower plant.

Matejče Monastery

The Monastery of the Most Holy Mother of God (Serbian: Манастир Пресвете Богородице), commonly known as Matejče (Матејче) or Matejić (Матејић), is a 14th-century Serbian Orthodox monastery located in the village of Matejče on the slopes of Skopska Crna Gora, near Skopje and Kumanovo. The village is inhabited by 89% Muslim Albanians and 10% Orthodox Serbs (2002 census). The monastery was built in the 14th century on the ruins of an older, Byzantine Greek church built in 1057–59, evident in preserved Greek inscriptions.[1][2] It was mentioned for the first time in 1300 in a chrysobull of Serbian king Stefan Milutin (r. 1282–1321). In the mid-14th century, Serbian emperor Stefan Dušan (r. 1331–55) started reconstructing the monastery, finished by his son Stefan Uroš V in 1357 (becoming his endowment). Coins of Uros V has been found at the site.[3] Isaiah the Serb and Vladislav Gramatik lived in the monastery. In the 18th century the roof was removed by the Ottomans and put on the Eski Mosque in Kumanovo, after which it deteriorated. In 1926–34 the monastery was renovated.

They are located in the village of Sllupcan otherwise also known to be the biggest village of the municipality of Lipkovo. The cementry are located at the beginning of the village close to villages stadium. Ismet Jashari monument

Ismet Jashari, also known as Komandant Kumanova was an Albanian member of the UCK movement who was killed on 25 August 1998 in Klecka Kosovo. The Ismet Jashari-Kumanova Brigade of the KLA is named in his memory. Jashari had left Switzerland where he lived to join the UCK to fight against Serbian military and police forces under the command of former Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic. In 2011 a monument dedicated to him was inaugurated in Lipkovo Municipality, Republic of Macedonia. The monument is located in Orizare otherwise it's also the place where Ismet Jashari was born and raised.The monument is located in the center of the village it's one of the first places visitors that come to lipkovo stop to visit because the village is also located in the middle of the municipality.The monument is also a very prised possession for the citizens of the village,who are very proud of their compatriot.
